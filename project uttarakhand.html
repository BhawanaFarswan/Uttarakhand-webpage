About Uttarakhand-The land of Gods!!!

Uttarakhand, formerly Uttaranchal, state of India, located in the northwestern part of the country.

On November 9, 2000, the state of Uttaranchal—the 27th state of India—was carved out of Uttar Pradesh.

In January 2007 the new state changed its name to Uttarakhand, meaning “northern region,” which was the traditional name for the area.

It's area is about 19,739 square miles (51,125 square km)

Districts in uttarakahand

Uttarakhand state is divided into two administrative divisions namely Kumaon and Garhwal. Garhwal region has 7 districts and Kumaon region has 6 districts. There are 13 districts in all in Uttarakhand.

Garhwal Division

Chamoli- Chamoli is a district blessed with stunning natural beauty and religious significance. It is home to several pilgrimage sites, including Badrinath and Hemkund Sahib. Chamoli is also known for its scenic valleys, snow-capped peaks, and trekking routes.

Dehradun - Dehradun is the capital city of Uttarakhand and is known for its pleasant climate, scenic beauty, and educational institutions. It is surrounded by the Himalayan ranges and offers a perfect blend of nature, history, and modern development.

Haridwar - Haridwar is a major pilgrimage destination for Hindus. It is located on the banks of the holy river Ganges and is considered one of the seven holiest places in Hinduism. The city is known for its religious significance, festivals, and the famous Kumbh Mela.

Pauri - Pauri is a district known for its scenic landscapes, lush forests, and majestic mountains. It is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The district offers trekking trails, wildlife sanctuaries, and a peaceful environment.

Rudraprayag - Rudraprayag is a district where the holy rivers Alaknanda and Mandakini meet. It is considered a sacred place for Hindus. Rudraprayag offers a serene environment, beautiful landscapes, and the opportunity to explore nearby religious sites.

New Tehri - New Tehri is known for the Tehri Dam, one of the highest dams in the world. The district offers stunning views of the Tehri Lake and surrounding hills. It is a hub for adventure activities like water sports, trekking, and camping.

Uttarkashi - Uttarkashi is a district situated on the banks of the holy river Bhagirathi. It is a gateway to many important religious sites and trekking destinations. Uttarkashi is known for its spiritual ambiance, ancient temples, and serene natural surroundings.

Kumaun Division

Almora - Almora is a charming hill town known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. It is located in the Kumaon region and offers panoramic views of the Himalayas. Almora is famous for its ancient temples, wildlife sanctuaries, and traditional handicrafts.

Bageshwar - Bageshwar is a district known for its religious and natural significance. It is home to several ancient temples, including the Bagnath Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The district is surrounded by scenic beauty, offering opportunities for trekking, camping, and exploring the wilderness.

Champawat - Champawat is a district located in the eastern part of Uttarakhand. It is known for its historical importance and natural beauty. The district is home to ancient temples, forts, and picturesque landscapes. Champawat offers a serene environment and is a gateway to the Himalayan ranges.

Nainital - Nainital is a popular hill station and a picturesque lake town in Uttarakhand. It is known for its beautiful Naini Lake, surrounded by lush green mountains. The town offers breathtaking views, pleasant weather, and various outdoor activities, making it a favorite tourist destination.

Pithoragarh - Pithoragarh is a picturesque district located in the eastern part of Uttarakhand. It is known for its breathtaking views of the Himalayas, high-altitude lakes, and scenic beauty. The district is also famous for its historical forts and cultural heritage.

Udham Singh Nagar - Udham Singh Nagar is an industrial district in Uttarakhand. It is known for its agricultural and industrial development. The district has several small and medium-scale industries and is a major contributor to the state’s economy. Udham Singh Nagar also offers natural beauty and historical sites to explore.

Culture of Uttarakhand

The culture of Uttarakhand still revolves around its traditional ethics, moral values, the simplicity of nature and a rich mythology.

Uttarakhand has a 'PAHADI' culture. Folk dance, music and festivals are a huge part of Uttarakhand culture.The land is blessed with beauty of himalayas and ancient temples.

It has been the centre of religious and spritual knowledge for ages.Its history and culture are deeply connected with religion and celebrated everywhere.

About Religion

A major section of the people in Uttarakhand are Hindus. Dances like Barada Nati, Bhotiya Dance, Chancheri, Chhapeli, Choliya Dance, Jagars, Jhora, Langvir Dance, Langvir Nritya, Pandav Nritya, Ramola, Shotiya Tribal Folk Dances, Thali-Jadda, and Jhainta are performed on various occasions in Uttarakhand.

The primary role of women is seen during the festivals as they are involved in preparing traditional dishes and singing folk songs.

Women are dressed traditionally in Ghagra-Choli with a Rangwali (veil), these women look beautiful and their beauty is further enhanced by the big Nose Rings that are made of gold.

Source of economy

Uttarakhand is largely dependent on its agriculture for the revenue.

,,

Basmati rice, wheat, soybeans, groundnuts, coarse cereals, pulses, and oil seeds are the most widely grown crops in the state.

There are also fruits like apples, oranges, pears, peaches, litchis, and plums that are widely grown in the state and bring a good amount of revenue.

Other key industries for the source of economy include tourism and hydropower, and recently development in IT, ITES, biotechnology, pharmaceuticals etc.
